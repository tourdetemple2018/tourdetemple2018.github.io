Safety Resources

In order for those participating in the Tour de Temple to get the most of their experience, all should familiarize themselves with bicycle safety tips. The following are but a few safety tips that will help ensure that the ride goes smoothly. It is encouraged that those planning on participating in the official Tour de Temple ride practice these tips and make them a habit prior to the ride:

  • Ride on the right side of the street (the same direction as traffic)
  • Stay in bike lanes
  • Avoid roads without good bike lanes
  • Stop at all red lights
  • Look both ways before turning
  • Watch for parked cars (and stay out of the "door zone")
  • Always wear a helmet and bright clothing (neon or flourescent)
  • Use hand-turn signals
  • Use lights when dark (early morning, evening, and night)
  • Keep hands on handlebars
  • Keep a safe distance from other bikes in front of you (1 to 2 bike lengths distance)
  • Only pass on left side (say "Passing on left" or use bell horn if available)
  • Be extra cautious in intersections / as cars turn right in front of you

Other safety considerations specific to the Tour de Temple activity:

  • Adult in front and back of each riding group
  • "Pit crew" support vehicles along route to help with any bike issues
  • Medical support present
  • Adults along path to direct turns in key areas

Further reading and additional resources: National Highway Traffic Safety Administration | National Safety Council | AAA Exchange
